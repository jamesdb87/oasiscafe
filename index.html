-Jim Lovell speaking from the moon on Apollo 8-

O.A.S.I.S.C.A.F.E. is an art installation currently in the planning and initial building stages. The installation will be a full size, roughly to scale, artistic representation of the Apollo Lunar Module that first landed on the moon in 1969. Visitors will be able to climb inside the installation for a fully immersive experience. The lower half, also known as the Descent Stage, will house various sub-installations by guest artists, and other works related to the theme of the piece. The upper half, or Ascent Stage, will be constructed to look and feel similar to the actual Lunar Module, so you will be able to see and experience what the astronauts lived in while on the moon.

Each letter of the title represents the name of one of the manned Lunar Modules that flew in space:

ORION – Apollo 16, Apr 1972
AQUARIUS – Apollo 13, Apr 1970
SPIDER – Apollo 9, Mar 1969
INTREPID – Apollo 12, Nov 1969
SNOOPY – Apollo 10, May 1969
CHALLENGER – Apollo 17, Dec 1972
ANTARES – Apollo 14, Feb 1971
FALCON – Apollo 15, July 1971
EAGLE – Apollo 11, July 1969

Our Mission is to convey and remember the biggest and most daring adventure humanity has ever taken. It is to allow the younger generations to connect with and understand what has just been a fact of history that happened before they were born. It’s celebrating something that was accomplished 50 years ago, that humanity will still be remembering and celebrating 1000 years from now.

O.A.S.I.S.C.A.F.E. will be at the Element 11 festival in Utah in June 2019, and also at Burning Man in Nevada’s Black Rock Desert in August of the same year. We are planning to have the installation on display in Salt Lake City in July, and are working with various organizations to determine the best location. More details to come.

This project is the dream and passion of Jeremy S Smith. He got his start as an artist a decade ago learning and creating fire for the Mantis art car in 2010, and then taking things to the next level the following year on the Scorpion art car fire system. In more recent years Jeremy has created his own large fiery installations for the Regional Burn in Utah and Burning Man. Prior to discovering his artistic side, Jeremy had a long bike racing career winning many races including the 2003 Lotoja, the longest 1-day bike race in North America, and the 180 mile 2008 Tour de Park City. Jeremy is still an avid cross county skier in winter and has competed in the 52km American Birkebeiner for the last 23 consecutive years with his dad. Jeremy is a Mechanical Engineer by profession and avid space enthusiast.

A project of this size takes an incredible crew. The crew consists of around 20 talented and dedicated community members excited to bring this project to life. Many more details to come on these amazing people as we get into the build phase in the coming weeks.
